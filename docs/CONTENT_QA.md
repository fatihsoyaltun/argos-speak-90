# Argos Speak 90 Content QA

## Level Progression

- Days 1-14: A1 foundation and confidence. The focus is short daily-life answers, self-introduction, routines, simple preferences, plans, and small problems.
- Days 15-42: Stronger A2 speaking patterns. The learner practices clearer details, comparisons, help requests, simple explanations, work/study updates, and short past experiences.
- Days 43-70: Early B1 explanations. The content adds reasons, examples, context, short stories, problem/solution language, and practical work or study communication.
- Days 71-90: More confident short speaking. The learner gives summaries, opinions, recommendations, next steps, and everyday/work answers with more structure.

## What Was Improved

- Replaced the old generic vocabulary padding with stage-aware speaking chunks.
- Reduced exact repeated filler phrases across nearby days.
- Kept each day at 8 practical vocabulary items while making the extra items match the day range more closely.
- Made Days 15-42 speaking prompts require 3-5 sentence answers instead of very short generic responses.
- Made Days 43-90 speaking prompts require 4-6 sentence answers with context, detail, reason, example, or next step.
- Aligned self-check wording with the stronger output targets in later phases.

## Known Limitations

- Audio timing and playback quality depend on the TTS provider and network response.
- The review checks remain intentionally simple and exact-answer based, so they are useful for active recall but not full language assessment.
- Some spaced repetition is intentional, especially for high-value speaking chunks, but exact phrase repetition should be monitored during real use.
- The app is still local-first; progress and practice text are not synced across devices.

## What To Watch During Real Use

- Whether a day feels too easy or too long on phone.
- Whether the vocabulary items feel useful in actual speaking, not just readable.
- Whether later speaking prompts push enough output without becoming intimidating.
- Whether review answers feel connected to the day’s listening, words, and speaking task.
- Whether repeated chunks feel helpful as spaced repetition or boring as filler.
